10 Expert Tips: From Famous Interior Designers How To Make Your Kitchen Look Bigger

  • Facebook
  • Instagram
  • Yelp
  • Houzz

When it comes to small kitchens, making them appear larger can be a challenge. However, with the right design techniques and strategies, it is possible to create the illusion of a larger space. The following 10 expert tips have been curated from highly regarded interior designers in the California region, and can help make your small kitchen look bigger.

1. Replace A Solid Roof With A Glazed One

A glazed roof can provide natural light into the kitchen, making the space feel more spacious. According to interior designer Kelly Wearstler, a roof that brings in more natural light can have a transformative effect on a space, making it feel larger and brighter.

2. Plan The Kitchen Layout To The Inch

Careful planning is key to maximizing space in a small kitchen. Interior designer Brian Patrick Flynn suggests planning the kitchen layout to the inch, taking into account the exact measurements of all appliances and cabinets to ensure that every inch of space is utilized efficiently.

3. Choose White Cabinetry To Fool Square Footage

White cabinetry can help make a kitchen appear larger. According to interior designer Justina Blakeney, white reflects light, making a space feel brighter and more spacious.

4. Stick To Light-Enhancing Neutrals

In addition to white cabinetry, sticking to light-enhancing neutrals such as light gray, beige, and pale blue can help make a small kitchen appear larger. Interior designer Erin Gates suggests using a neutral palette to create a harmonious and cohesive look that will help to open up the space.

5. Keep Dark Colors To Base Cabinetry Where Possible

While light-enhancing neutrals are important, adding a pop of color can also make a kitchen feel more interesting. Interior designer Emily Henderson suggests keeping dark colors to the base cabinetry, where they won’t overpower the space.

6. Balance Out Dark Cabinetry With Pale Countertops

To balance out dark cabinetry, it is important to choose pale countertops. Interior designer Sarah Sherman Samuel suggests using light-colored countertops, such as marble or quartz, to brighten up the space and create a clean and modern look.

7. Create Visual Continuity With To-The-Ceiling Cabinetry

To make a small kitchen appear taller, interior designer Joanna Gaines suggests installing cabinetry that goes all the way to the ceiling. This creates a continuous visual line, making the space feel taller and more spacious.

8. Widen The Room With Wall Shelves Not Cabinetry

Adding cabinetry to a small kitchen can make it feel cluttered. To avoid this, interior designer Leanne Ford suggests using wall shelves instead. Wall shelves can provide storage without taking up valuable floor space, and they also help to widen the room, making it feel more spacious.

9. Consider Freestanding Furniture Over Fitted

Fitted furniture can make a small kitchen feel cramped. Interior designer Studio McGee suggests considering freestanding furniture, such as a kitchen island or a standalone pantry, which can provide storage and create a more open and airy feel.

10. Stretch Floor Space With Light-Colored, Large Format Floor Tiles

Finally, choosing the right flooring can help make a small kitchen feel larger. Interior designer Jennifer Morris suggests using light-colored, large format floor tiles, which can create a seamless look that will stretch the appearance of the space.

By incorporating these tips and strategies, small kitchens can be transformed into bright, airy, and spacious spaces. Whether you are working with an interior designer or undertaking a kitchen renovation yourself, these tips can help you to make the most of your small kitchen and create a beautiful space.
